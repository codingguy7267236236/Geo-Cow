Shoreline Management Plan (SMP)

Holderness Coast

Facts

  • 2.3k km of 17k km being defended
  • UK's largest coastline made up of multiple sediment cells

Impacts

  • 200 years 200 homes at risk
  • Homes and business at risk of being "swollowed" by the sea
  • Loss of habitat and livlihoods

SMP

  • Do nothing at Flamborough head due to highly resistant chalk + sheltered bay
  • Hold the line, sea wall and concrete revetments at Hornsea due to businesses
  • Do nothing at Hornsea caravan park, increased erosion
  • Concrete revetments and sea wall at Easington due to power plant providing 25% of UK coal fire energy

Odisha

Facts

  • Manage coastline in sustainable manner
  • Balance, social, environmental and economic conflicts

Opportunities

  • Tourism and eco-tourism jobs generated from maintaining beaches and wildlife parks to preserve wildlife
  • Bird sanctuary at Chilka home to 150 unique bird species + jobs
  • Locals employed into fishing and agriculture to catch resources

Risks

  • Man made changes such as hard engineering strategies leading to accelrated erosion which is changing morphology and flow of system
  • 8% (40km) of coastline prone to high erosion
  • Retreating coastline which is home to a majority of Indias population

Adaptation and Mitigating Sea Level Change

Kiribati

Risks

  • By 2100 Islands to be fully submerged
  • By 2100 50% magroves to be lost
  • 7M in danger of losing homes + livelihoods
  • Limited resources

Mitigation

  • Increasing Atol height by builidng coral sea wall
  • Planting Mangrove forest of 50k mangroves to reduce sea level rise as mangroves absorb lots of water

Adaptation

  • Micro-payments to allow people to create new businesses and services to move away from fishing industry
  • Migration with dignity to encourage migration to Fiji
  • President Atone bought 6k acres of farming land in Fiji to use for growing crops
