Hazards

In hazards I recommend from experience picking out 2 case studies from each natural event. Pick 1 high income country example and 1 low income country example for each; to allow for comparisons and global scale when answering exam questions. I recommend studying Haiti earthquake and hurricane Matthew because it covers earthquakes, tropical storms and multi-hazardous components; which this saves you learning an additional case study such as Typhoon Haiyan.

Below are examples of what to look for when learning your case studies. Following this structure should give you enough knowledge to answer exam questions. What you will need to do from here is develop points based around these facts.

    • Plate Theories

    Gravitational sliding, Ridge push Slab Pull

    • 1965 Tuzo-Wilson discovered conservative plate boundary
    • 1966 McKenzie suggested 2 layers of mantle controlled movement of tectonic plates
    • Con: didn't investigate subduction theory

    Sea Floor Spreading

    • Came about by Tharps discovery of mid ocean ridge
    • Harry Hess (naval officer) mapped ocean floor with sonar
    • Concluded ridges and rift valleys were result of tectonic plate movements
    • Ocean trenches showed ocean floor subduction
    • Con: No geo-physical evidence
    • Con: Couldn't explain plate destruction and recycle

    Jigsaw Theory - Francis Bacon

    • Predicted plates fit together like a jigsaw
    • Con: Missing gaps
    • Gaps explained by erosion, deposition, changes in sea and land levels

    Palaeomagnetism

    • Vince Matthews discovered symmetrical pattern of magnetic strips either side of mid ocean ridges
    • Sea floor dated, strips of equal distance from centre of ridge same age
    • Showed earths polarity changes every 400k years
    • Magnite in lava errupted onto ocean floor recording earths magnetic orientation at the time

    Continental Drift - Wegner

    • Found dinosaur fossils
    • Super continent "Pangea" predicted to existed 300M years ago
    • Glacial deposits - proved Pangea super continent existed
    • Criticism: Didn't know why - non conclusive

    Hazard Management Models

    Hazard Management Cycle

    • Pro - simple for anyone to understand
    • Pro - easy to plan
    • Pro - saves more lives than doing nothing
    • Pro - Feedback helps them improve next time

    Park Model

    • Pro - shows how quality of life can be improved
    • Pro - provides time scale
    • Pro - Illustrates impacts

    • Con - hard to interpret for LICs
    • Con - vastly different for each event
