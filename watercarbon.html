Water & Carbon

London Basin

Facts

  • Over abstraction from brewing industry led to water levels dropped 88m below sea level
  • 2.6bn litres for 9m population
  • 250mm of 690mm useable rainfall
  • Water levels recovering 3m per year since 1990 due to less economic activity
  • Mid 60s brewing industry replaced by commerce industry

River Eden

Facts

  • Added meander to increased water stored
  • North-west England
  • Between mountains of Lake District and Pennines

Water Cycle

  • Rainfall higher than national average due to relief of area, lots of water enters river channels
  • Basin long + narrow, increases lag time
  • Infiltration quick so little surface run-off, higher lag time, water stored increases
  • Intense farming impacts soil which reduces infiltration so more surface runoff + flooding
  • Grazing upland areas reduced vegetation intercepted so more water reaches rivers

Carbon Cycle

  • Move from vegetation farming to cattle farming, more CO2 from animals
  • Less CO2 from forest areas being cleared for cattle farming and housing
  • Housing developments on floodplains requiring defences which offset flooding

Amazon Rainforest

Facts

  • Largest Rainforest covering 40% South America Landmass
  • Hot and wet climate, desnse vegetation
  • Evaporation over Atlantic Ocean blown towards Amazon contribting to heavy rainfall
  • Dense canopy, causes high interception meaning less water flows into rivers

Water Cycle

  • Evaporation over Atlantic Ocean blown towards Amazon contribting to heavy rainfall
  • Dense canopy, causes high interception meaning less water flows into rivers
  • Deforestation reduced canopy, less rainfall interception so more water reaches surface and oceans + flood risks
  • Deforestation reduces evapotranspiration rates so less water vapour reaches atmosphere, more chance of drought

Carbon Cycle

  • Increased CO2 concentration increased photosynthesis rates leading to growth
  • Last 30yrs trees increased in height and taken up additional 500m tonnes of carbon per year
  • Stores 100bn tonnes of carbon
  • Some studies show trees dying quicker due to rapid growth
  • No roots to hold soil together so more carbon transferred to hydrosphere from soil
  • Less leaf litter, no humus formed so carbon stored limited
  • Less trees means the natural carbon shink becomes less efficient

Mitigation

  • Replanting of new trees to restore 3.2m hectares
  • Selective logging, some trees felled allows forest to regenerate/recover
  • Environmental laws, e.g. quota on logging wood from rainforest
  • Conservation with national parks
